Frequently asked

I can't hear the click.
Check the side switch on your iPhone — silent mode mutes the metronome. If the switch is off, raise media volume with the side buttons (not ringer volume). On Bluetooth headphones, confirm they're connected and selected as output.
The click feels late or early.
Open Settings → Latency Calibration and follow the on-screen tap test. This compensates for your specific output device's audio path.
How do I unlock Mixed Meter, Polyrhythm, or Human Feel?
These are part of the Pro upgrade — a one-time purchase, no subscription. Tap any locked feature to see the upgrade screen. If you've already purchased, tap Restore Purchases on the same screen.
How do I delete all my data?
Open All Settings → Delete All Data. This permanently removes your practice log, streaks, goals, setlists, and imported sounds. The action is immediate and cannot be undone.
MIDI Clock or Ableton Link isn't connecting.
Both features rely on local-network and Bluetooth permissions. Confirm both are allowed in iOS Settings → Metronome. For Ableton Link, devices must be on the same Wi-Fi network.
Volume buttons aren't changing the BPM.
Enable Volume Buttons = BPM in All Settings → BPM Controls. The toggle is off by default to avoid surprising users who expect normal volume behavior.
The app crashed or behaves strangely.
Force-quit and relaunch. If the issue persists, email us with your iPhone model, iOS version, and a brief description of what happened.

Privacy

Metronome does not collect, sell, or share your personal data. The app stores your settings, practice log, and setlists on your device only. Optional iCloud sync uses your private Apple iCloud account — the developer and any third party have no access.

Microphone access is used only by the built-in tuner to detect pitch in real time; audio is processed on-device and never recorded or transmitted.
